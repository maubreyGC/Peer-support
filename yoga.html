Yoga These free online yoga classes are for people affected by sexual misconduct. You don't need any yoga experience to join. Classes are trauma-informed, adaptable, and open to all. A mental health worker will be available if you need extra support. Registration is open and on an ongoing basis. This means you can sign up once and attend as many sessions as you like. Sign up using this form or email SMSRCPSP-CSRISPSP@forces.gc.ca

Who can join To participate, you must:
  • be a current or former Canadian Armed Forces (CAF) member, Veteran, Department of National Defence (DND) public service employee, Cadet, Junior Canadian Ranger, or part of the Defence Community
  • have experienced sexual misconduct during your service or in the defence environment
  • have access to a computer, tablet, or smartphone with a stable internet connection
  • have access to Microsoft Teams, either through the desktop/mobile app or a supported web browser (note: Safari is not supported)
  • for group services, be willing to take part in a virtual discussion with a Peer Support Program staff before the first group session
Trauma-informed yoga A gentle yoga class for all levels and conditions. No yoga experience required. Chair yoga options and posture modifications are available to support people with reduced mobility.
  • Duration: 75 minutes
  • Language: English and French
  • Hosted by: A certified yoga instructor
Schedule Sign up using this form or email SMSRCPSP-CSRISPSP@forces.gc.ca Yoga flow for beginners An easy and supportive introduction to yoga flow, practiced on a mat. Sessions include simple movements, steady pacing, and easy-to-follow instructions. Participants can choose movement variations that work best for their bodies. This class is designed to support relaxation, and build body awareness and confidence with basic yoga movements.
  • Duration: 60 minutes
  • Language: English and French
  • Hosted by: A certified yoga instructor and a peer supporter may be present
