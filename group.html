Support groups Our support groups provide a braver and confidential space to connect, share experiences, and learn coping and wellness strategies while exploring your healing journey. All sessions are free and confidential. Participants are required to sign up in advance of the first session. We invite you to sign up for one or more groups. Sign up using this form or email SMSRCPSP-CSRISPSP@forces.gc.ca

Who can join To participate, you must:
  • be a current or former Canadian Armed Forces (CAF) member, Veteran, Department of National Defence (DND) public service employee, Cadet, Junior Canadian Ranger, or part of the Defence Community
  • have experienced sexual misconduct during your service or in the defence environment
  • have access to a computer, tablet, or smartphone with a stable internet connection
  • have access to Microsoft Teams, either through the desktop/mobile app or a supported web browser (note: Safari is not supported)
  • for group services, be willing to take part in a virtual discussion with a Peer Support Program staff before the first group session
Learning Together peer support group This group combines learning with peer support, giving participants a chance to grow, heal, and support one another. Topics focus on empowerment, healing and growth through lived experiences.
  • Group duration: Up to 8 virtual sessions; 90 minutes each
  • Hosted by: A trained peer supporter and mental health worker
  • Group size: Up to 8 participants per group
  • Include the impacts of trauma, moral injury, setting boundaries, coping strategies, self-care, and more.
  • Commitment: Closed group, meaning no new participants can join after the first session. We recommend that participants attend all group sessions.
Schedule Sign up using this form or email SMSRCPSP-CSRISPSP@forces.gc.ca Peer Connect: Informal group Informal Groups are peer-led spaces. Participants come together to connect, share lived experiences, and engage in supportive conversations in a relaxed and informal setting. Discussions are led by participants' needs.
  • Group duration: Up to 8 virtual sessions; 90 minutes each
  • Hosted by: A trained peer supporter and a mental health worker
  • Group size: Up to 10 participants per group
  • Discussion topics: Discussions are guided by participants' needs. While participants are asked not to disclose personal details of their experiences, they are encouraged to freely discuss relevant topics.
  • Commitment: Closed group, meaning no new participants can join after the first session. Once signed up, participation is flexible; you can attend as many sessions as you like.
